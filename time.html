What time is it?

These are some common expressions to tell the time and the hour, but first we're going to need to learn the numbers.

One [1]

Üks

Two [2]

Kaks

Three [3]

Kolm

Four [4]

Neli

Five [5]

Viis

Six [6]

Kuus

Seven [7]

Seitse

Eight [8]

Kaheksa

Nine [9]

Uheksa

Ten [10]

Kümme

Eleven [11]

Uksteist

Twelve [12]

Kaksteist

A good way to get acquainted with the numbers in Estonian is to keep in mind that some of them are part of the construction of the word of a day, for example, Kolmapäev, Neljapäev

And now, let's get started with the time, here's how a casual conversation would be

Mis kell on?

What time is it?

Kell on üks

It's one o'clock

Here's a different answer

Kell on juba üks

It's already one o'clock

Here's another way to ask for the time, with a different answer.

Kui palju kell on?

What time is it?

Kell on pool seitse

It's half past six

And the last example, this is kinda tricky.

Kui palju kell on?

What time is it?

Uks hetk, ma kohe vaatan, veerand kolm

Just a moment, I'll have a look, quarter past two.

Here's another answer

Appi! Kell on juba kolmveerand kaksteist!

Oh dear! It's already quarter to twelve!
